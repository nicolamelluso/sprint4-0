    Additional Material

    Along with an additional activity, UNIPI proposes the partners to add also the one presented in the minutes, i.e. the short videos for supporting other teachers in the implementation of a SPRINT4.0 course.

    In the SPRINT4.0 model, every activity aimed at improving the actual status of a company towards the Industry 4.0 paradigm starts from an initial assessment (Audit Methodology) of the maturity level of the company.

    After the assessment several improvements can be identified, and a suitable strategy designed and implemented. The actions can be local improvements, can be the redesign of a process through the Lean Manufacturing methodologies or the adoption of one of the enabling technologies.

    For all the situations above an initial baseline has to be defined. It means decomposing each process and measuring the lead time (average and deviation) for each and any operations/processes. Usually in the past the baseline was obtained thanks to human being measuring with a chronometer the time spent by each operator in doing a specific operation. Such an analysis belongs to the so called “methods time measurement (mtm)”.

    If we refer to SMEs where only a fraction of the tasks is automatized, we can recognize how methods-time-measurement could be useful to assess a process, a procedure etc.. the measurement at t=t0 is the baseline. After the process has been redesigned and implemented the measurement can be repeated in order to quantify the effect of the improvement (if any).

    In an Industry 4.0 environment we can not think to continue to measure by using an employee, but we can use Industrial IOT systems to measure all the durations of tasks. Hand tools (jaws, screwdrivers, etc..), personal protective equipment (PPE) such as clothing, helmets, goggles, shoes, etc.. can be equipped with RFID tags while small machines (drill, hot glue gun, soldering machine, etc..) can be equipped with current and other basic sensors. The operator wears a glove equipped with a RFID reader able to read the RFID attached to tools or PPEs, the sensors located at the machine level are connected and a data capturing system collects all the data. Such a system is able to disambiguate each and every operation and assigns them the measured duration.

    Imagine an assembly line (or analogous process) where humans are involved with machines in the production of a good. In a few work-shifts the system is able to accumulate data and calculate parameters about all and each operation. Such an activity sets the baseline. The measurements can be repeated after an improvement, after a training activity, from time to time to see if any changes.

    The present activity seems to be high valuable and to have a high applicability to many different scenarios especially in the SMEs but also in Large Enterprises.

    The plan we present you is to give University of Pisa the responsibility of designing the overall methodology and the process with the support of TOI, especially for technical and technological issues, and to test/implement the methodology in the plants of Selettra. The result of this activity will be a set of training materials, for showing the students the results and explaining them how to replicate this model.

    As the course is already designed, UniPi is planning to present to the partnership the final output during the Pisa Meeting and test the training materials prepared during a “side event” with the students during the course. This side event will be structured as a 3 hours workshop, after which the other partners will be able to give suggestions, request of modifications, etc. If BIBA is interested in this side event, it could be also possible to hold it also in Germany. During the training course in Barcellona UniPi and TOI could present a first overview of the tools and methodology.

    The additional training materials developed could be also useful in the development of the prototype (after the company coaching), at least for the students participating in the side event.

    All the validated materials should be included in IO6 and could be adopted as common training materials for any following implementation of the SPRINT4.0 training course, both by the partner Universities and any other interested University"
