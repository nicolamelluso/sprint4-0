Brainstorming on IO5: Coaching methodologies (Technology coaching – Universities; Application oriented coaching – Companies) for empowering competencies and for elaborating prototypes according to Industry 4.0

The first question asked by Nicola (UniPi) is: what do the companies expect from the students at the end of the mobility?

  • --Enzo (Selettra): he expects the students to be able to provide ideas and solutions to solve the problems presented by the company during the challenge.
  • --Jordi (Barna Steel): he expects, at the end of the path, to have a proof of concept to be further developed, in other words a deeper analysis of the problem and a number of ideas for actually solving it.
  • --Carl (OHS) has the same objectives. He hopes the students will be able to work within the company with a good degree of autonomy and organize their work by themselves. For sure, he will provide them with all the support they need, but it is important for them to develop also autonomy as a skill.

Alan (URL) believes that the students should just solve a part of the problem, and provide for this part a proof of concept, transforming the pretotype presented in the competition and developed thanks to the Technology coaching (host University) into a prototype (thanks to the company coaching). The students should prove that the problem is solvable; the company will then have the chance for completing the work.

- - Technology Coaching (within the University): the output of this first kind of coaching should be a preliminary action/project plan, to be deployed for real during the transnational mobility within the company.

The Project Plan could be composed of:

  • --WPs/Phases with at least one output per each phase and/or list of tasks organized on a weekly base
  • --GANTT chart
  • --Expected effort expressed in time
  • --List of resources needed
  • --The expected impact in the company (improvements)

- - Application oriented coaching (within the Company): the output should be a proof of concept, to be further developed by the company. While in the Technology Coaching phase the students will plan the work to do (planning), in the Application oriented coaching they will implement the plan (doing).

During the mobility the students should also focus on business aspects, dedicating at least a couple of days for understanding and defining the business prospective of the prototype developed in the previous days. To do this, the students could be asked to carry out a SWOT analysis (or some more sophisticate tool) for defining how the company business could be changed by the introduction of the protoyped solution. Also the stakeholder analysis could be useful to understand the external relations that could be created in a following implementation phase

The results of this brainstorming should be conceived just as suggestion for the organization(s) responsible for preparing these methodologies (being Intootion/GP or other organizations in case they should leave the partnership).

As the first coaching will take place in September in Spain (duration: 1 month, after the end of the course and summer break, more info here), the methodologies should be ready by the end of July. A more detailed plan will be prepared when the decision about the partner organizations wil be taken.
